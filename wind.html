What is Wind Energy?


Wind energy is the process of converting the kinetic energy from wind into electrical power using wind turbines. It is a renewable and sustainable source of energy that harnesses the natural movement of air to generate electricity without emitting harmful pollutants or greenhouse gases. It is generated by harnessing the natural movement of air through wind turbines. As the wind blows, it causes the blades of the turbine to spin, converting the kinetic energy of the wind into mechanical power. This mechanical power is then transformed into electrical energy through a generator. Wind energy is considered one of the cleanest and most sustainable energy sources because it does not produce greenhouse gas emissions, pollution, or deplete natural resources. Wind farms can be installed both onshore and offshore, contributing to the global shift toward greener energy solutions.
